Section 01

Why Mathematical Constants?

In most token projects, parameters are guesses — developers pick round numbers that look clean or feel safe. We rejected this. Mathematical constants are immutable, irrefutable, and universal. They don't need justification because nature already provided it.

When we set the buy tax at 2.718%, we didn't pick 3% because it was easy or 2.5% because it felt balanced. We chose e — Euler's number — because it is the base of the natural exponential function, the rate at which compound growth occurs in nature. There is a poetic logic to using the constant that governs natural growth to govern the rate at which our prize pool grows.

When we set the sell tax at 3.141%, we chose π — Pi — the ratio of a circle's circumference to its diameter, perhaps the most universally recognised constant in mathematics. It is transcendental, irrational, and infinite. So is the precision with which it is encoded into the contract.

Prize Pool State Machine

The prize pool is not a static bucket. It is a four-state machine that automatically adjusts tax routing to protect its floor and prevent runaway accumulation.

Normal State

Active Operation

Pool sits between 5% and 7.5% of total supply. The system operates normally: buy tax flows to pool, sell 1.141% (π − 2) flows to pool. Prizes cost 50% from pool + 50% via market buy of 52F.

Pool: 5% ≤ x ≤ 7.5% of supply
Flush State

Overaccumulation Guard

Pool exceeds 7.5% of supply — too large relative to token supply. Buy tax is redirected to liquidity. Sell 1.141% (π − 2) also redirects to liquidity. Prizes are paid 100% from pool only. This drains the pool back to target range.

Pool: > 7.5% of supply → re-routes tax
Floor State

Hard Floor Protection

Pool reaches 5% of supply — the initial seed level. All tax revenue is now directed to refilling the pool. Prizes are suspended until the tax accumulation gate is satisfied. The pool can never be depleted below its starting level.

Pool = 5% of supply → prizes suspended
Flush Exit

Automatic Recovery

When a flush state pool drains back below 5% of supply, the system automatically resumes normal tax routing. No admin action required — the contract detects the threshold crossing and adjusts on the next transaction.

Pool drops below 5% → normal routing resumes
Tax Accumulation Gate

Before a prize triggers, accumulated tax in the current cycle must equal at least 50% of the prize value. This gate is the primary sustainability mechanism: it mathematically prevents pool drain at all volume levels. At low volume, prizes are rarer but the pool stays healthy. At high volume, prizes flow freely but always proportionally to incoming tax.

Section 05

The Birthday Paradox Engine

The lottery is not random. It is a deterministic collision detection system inspired by one of probability theory's most elegant results.

The Birthday Paradox states: in a room of just 23 people, the probability that two share a birthday exceeds 50%. The maths seems counterintuitive until you realise you're counting pairs, not individuals.

We applied this principle to transaction hashes. Every buy transaction generates a hash — a unique 256-bit fingerprint. We take that hash, reduce it modulo 52, and map it to one of 52 slots in a ring buffer. Of those 52 slots, 8 are active — the Fibonacci positions: slots 1, 2, 3, 5, 8, 13, 21, and 34.

If your transaction hash maps to a Fibonacci slot, you've had a collision — your hash "collides" with an active position. Subject to passing the tax accumulation gate, you win a prize.

Collision Probability

With 8 active slots of 52 total, each qualifying buy transaction has a 8/52 ≈ 15.38% base probability of mapping to a Fibonacci slot. The actual prize rate is lower due to the tax gate, but the mathematical certainty of collisions over time is what makes this a provably fair system — not a casino, but a mathematical inevitability.

Section 10

Common Questions

Can the team change the tax rates or constants after launch?
No. The mathematical constants are hard-coded at deployment. There are no admin functions that can alter e, π, φ, or any of the Fibonacci slot positions. The contract is immutable in these respects. What is written is written.
What happens to the prize pool if nobody wins for a long time?
It grows. Tax revenue continues routing to the pool on every buy and sell. The flush state guard then kicks in when the pool exceeds 7.5% of supply, at which point tax redirects to liquidity instead. The system is self-managing — it cannot grow unchecked, and it cannot be depleted.
How do I know the collision result isn't manipulated?
Randomness is sourced from pallet-vrf on QF Network, derived from block entropy two epochs prior to the current block. A validator would need to retroactively alter sealed blockchain state to manipulate results — computationally infeasible on a PoS chain. The Fibonacci slot positions are fixed in code, publicly verifiable on-chain.
Why are sell transactions excluded from the lottery?
To align incentives with project health. Including sells would incentivise rapid sell-buy cycles to farm collision chances. By restricting eligibility to buys only, the lottery rewards genuine participation and long-term holding, not trading velocity. It also prevents wash-trading attacks on the prize mechanism.
Is this a ponzi scheme or pyramid structure?
No. Ponzi schemes require new capital to pay existing participants. Project 52F's prize pool is pre-seeded at launch (5% of supply, owned by the contract) and replenished by transaction tax — not by recruitment or new investor capital. The system can function even with zero new buyers as long as holders trade.
What is the minimum holding to win a prize?
At the launch tier: just 52 tokens. Each tier requires a holding minimum that is a multiple of 52 (52, 520, 5,200, 52,000). The minimum is checked at time of collision. You must hold the minimum in your wallet at the block of the winning transaction.
