04

STORED IN THE ROLLING ARRAY

Your value and address are written to the next slot in the 52-entry ring buffer. The write head advances with every qualifying tx. When slot 52 is reached it wraps to 0. Fibonacci-index slots (1,2,3,5,8,13,21,34) glow live for collision.

Each win pays 0.52% of the current balance, not a fixed amount. The pool mathematically cannot reach zero. Buy tax (e = 2.718%) continuously replenishes it. Self-sustaining in perpetuity without any admin action.

TAX STRUCTURE
BUY 2.718% (e)  ·  SELL 3.141% (π)  ·  ANCHORED TO CONSTANTS
▼

Every DeFi token has taxes. Most pick numbers arbitrarily — 3%, 5%, 10%. 52F is different: the taxes are derived from transcendental constants that appear throughout mathematics and nature.

BUY TAX
2.718%
EULER’S NUMBER (e) — 2.71828…
100% → Birthday Paradox Prize Pool
SELL TAX
3.141%
PI (π) — 3.14159…
1.000% → Founders & Team

Why e for the buy tax? Euler’s number is the base of natural growth — e governs compound interest, population growth, radioactive decay. It’s the most “natural” rate of change. Every buy feeds the prize pool at exactly e%, the rate of natural growth. This is also intentionally low — below the sell tax — to encourage buying activity.

Why π for the sell tax? Pi is irrational, transcendental, and appears in circular and cyclical systems. The sell tax at π% is slightly higher than the buy tax, providing gentle sell-side friction. Crucially, the sell tax routes to two different destinations — team and the Drip-Feed vault — not to the prize pool, so sellers don’t directly fuel the game.

Note: The difference between π (3.141%) and e (2.718%) is 0.423% — this asymmetry is intentional. Sells are slightly more expensive than buys, providing mild upward price pressure without being punitive.

Why 2.141% to the Drip-Feed vault from sell tax? The 2.141% portion is π minus 1 (3.141 − 1.000 = 2.141). The 1% going to the team is a clean integer; the remainder, being irrational, routes to the vault. Even the split respects mathematical elegance.

Why depth-triggered rather than time-triggered? Time-based drips (e.g. “release 1% every week”) are blunt instruments — they add supply regardless of whether the market needs it. A depth trigger is smarter: supply only enters when liquidity is thin, directly solving the problem. This prevents both supply scarcity (too little depth) and supply flooding (releasing when depth is already healthy).

What is the Inventory Floor? A minimum token balance maintained in the pool. When pool reserves fall below 5% of their target, the contract calculates exactly how many tokens are needed to restore the floor, swaps them for QF on SPIN-Swap, then adds both sides as LP. The protocol always provides equal-value liquidity — never just dumping tokens.

Note: The Drip-Feed vault is the only source of new circulating supply post-launch. There is no team airdrop, no marketing allocation, no “ecosystem fund” that can dump on holders. Every token entering circulation comes through the liquidity engine or sell tax routing.

VOLATILITY DAMPENER
±6.52% TRIGGER  ·  520-BLOCK TWAP  ·  STANDBY / FREEZE MODES
▼

The Dampener is an on-chain circuit breaker. It continuously computes a 520-block TWAP (Time-Weighted Average Price) from the 52F/QF pair and monitors price deviations. When the price moves too far too fast, it acts.

TWAP WINDOW

TRIGGER THRESHOLD

DAMPENER WALLET

Why 6.52% as the trigger? 6 is a perfect number (the smallest positive integer equal to the sum of its proper divisors: 1+2+3=6). Appending 52 — the protocol identity — gives 6.52%. This threshold is tight enough to catch genuine manipulation attempts but loose enough to allow normal price discovery. A 5% swing in 52 seconds is almost certainly wash trading or a coordinated attack.

What happens when triggered? The Dampener sells a portion of its 5% wallet into QF on SPIN-Swap, then uses both the QF proceeds and an equivalent token amount to add LP. This pushes the price back toward the TWAP while simultaneously deepening the pool — making future manipulation more expensive.

Why 520 blocks for the TWAP? 520 = 52 × 10. At 0.1s block time, this is exactly 52 seconds — fast enough to catch rapid price manipulation but slow enough to smooth out normal transaction noise. A 520-block TWAP on a QF Network chain is roughly equivalent to a 90-minute TWAP on Ethereum — a proven oracle security window.

Standby and Freeze modes: If the SPIN-Swap pool is too shallow to compute a reliable TWAP (insufficient reserves), the Dampener enters Standby mode — it stops trading but continues monitoring. If pool depth remains insufficient for an extended period, it enters Freeze mode — the entry gate falls back to the minimum (270.4 QF) to encourage buys that will restore pool depth.

Important: The Dampener wallet is ring-fenced — it can only perform its one function. It cannot be drained, repointed, or used to pay team expenses. Its only interaction is with the SPIN-Swap pair.

Why 0.052% of reserves? 52 is the protocol identity, and 0.052% = 52 / 100,000. As the pool grows, the minimum buy scales proportionally — this means the barrier to entry remains proportional to the pool’s actual value. Early buyers face a lower absolute QF cost; as the ecosystem matures the gate naturally rises, filtering for more committed participants.

Why a floor of 270.4 QF? This is 5.2 QF (the original design value) scaled ×52 for QF Network’s supply ratio. 5.2 = 52 ÷ 10. Why a ceiling of 2,704 QF? 2,704 = 52 × 52 = 52². The ceiling is the square of the protocol identity. This creates a natural upper bound while ensuring Slot 52 — the Champion’s Seat — requires exactly 52² QF to claim.

Claiming it requires 2,704 × φ ≈ 4,374 QF. This makes the Champion’s Seat aspirational without being mathematically arbitrary.

Why does this matter? A fixed-price gate (e.g. always 270.4 QF) would become either trivially cheap or prohibitively expensive as QF price changes. The dynamic gate keeps the entry cost economically consistent relative to the pool’s actual value at all times.

PRIZE POOL ENGINE
0.52% PER WIN  ·  ASYMPTOTIC DECAY  ·  SELF-SUSTAINING  ·  ZERO ADMIN
▼

The prize pool is the heart of Project 52F. It is funded at launch with 5% of supply and replenished continuously by the e% buy tax. It pays out on every collision win. It cannot be drained to zero.

Why 0.52% per win? 52 is the protocol identity. 0.52% = 52 / 10,000. This value was chosen because it produces meaningful payouts while ensuring the pool cannot be depleted quickly. If the pool is at 100 tokens and a win occurs, only 0.52 tokens leave — 99.48 remain. The next win also takes 0.52% of what’s left, and so on: the pool asymptotically approaches but never reaches zero.

Why split 50/50? The Birthday Paradox requires two participants. Both contributed equally to the collision — one by entering the array, one by matching it. An equal split respects the symmetry of the mechanism. A different split (e.g. 70/30) would create a “maker” and “taker” dynamic that doesn’t reflect the underlying probability math.

What is asymptotic decay and why does it matter? If each win takes a fixed percentage of the remaining balance, the pool follows the curve P(n) = P(0) × (1 − 0.0052)^n. After 1,000 wins, the pool retains (0.9948)^1000 ≈ 0.6% of its original value — still non-zero. In practice, buy tax inflows outpace wins, so the pool grows over time with healthy volume.

The Readiness Meter: The fill percentage displayed on the dashboard is the ratio of current pool balance to its balance at the last collision reset. It fills continuously via buy tax. When it hits 100%, it simply means the pool has returned to its pre-last-win size. There is no mechanical change at 100% — it is a community engagement signal.

Design intent: The prize pool is designed to be self-sustaining without any admin intervention. No multisig. No emergency drain. No pause function for payouts. The contract is written so that once deployed, the prize pool operates autonomously forever.

▼

The first 6,000 blocks after deployment (10 minutes at 0.1s) are subject to launch protection. These fuses exist to prevent bots, snipers, and coordinated attacks from capturing disproportionate supply at launch.

Why 6,000 blocks? 6,000 × 0.1 seconds = 600 seconds = 10 minutes. This gives human participants enough time to read and understand the launch before bots can sweep the available supply. The number 6,000 also echoes the 6% max wallet ceiling — consistent numerical language.

Why does the purchase limit decay? Rather than a hard on/off at block 6,000, the 5% purchase ceiling linearly decays to unrestricted over the blockade period. This means very early buyers face a 5% cap, buyers at block 3,000 face a ~2.5% cap, and by block 6,000 there is no cap. The decay prevents a sudden “open floodgates” event.

Why 6% max wallet? 6 is a perfect number. A 6% wallet ceiling prevents any single address from accumulating enough supply to meaningfully manipulate price. At 6%, the largest possible holder represents a 94% free float — sufficient for genuine decentralisation. This limit persists after launch.

Why a 52-block cooldown? 52 blocks = 5.2 seconds. This creates a minimum gap between trades from the same address, making sandwich attacks and MEV strategies economically impractical. A bot that can only trade every 5.2 seconds has its attack throughput reduced by orders of magnitude.

After launch: Once the 6,000-block blockade period ends, only the 6% max wallet and 52-block cooldown remain active permanently. There are no other transfer restrictions. The protocol does not pause, freeze, or block any address after the launch window.
